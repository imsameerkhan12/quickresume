Declaration in Resume: Format, Samples, and Whether You Need It

The declaration is the line at the bottom of many Indian resumes stating that everything above is true, followed by your name, date and place. Here's the honest answer nobody gives: for most private-sector jobs in 2026, it's optional — but there are cases where you should still include it.

When you SHOULD include a declaration

  • Government job applications, PSUs, and anything with a formal application format
  • Academic institutions and teaching posts
  • When the employer's format explicitly asks for it
  • Older/traditional companies where HR expects the classic format

When you can skip it

  • IT companies, startups, MNCs — modern recruiters neither expect nor read it
  • When space is tight: one more project or skill line beats a declaration

Standard declaration formats (copy one)

I hereby declare that the information provided above is true to the best of my knowledge and belief.

Place: Mumbai
Date: ____________      (Your Name)
I solemnly declare that all the information furnished in this resume is free of errors to the best of my knowledge.

Place: Delhi
Date: ____________      Signature
All the details mentioned above are true to the best of my knowledge, and I take full responsibility for their correctness.

(Your Name)
✅ Keep it to one sentence. The name, place and date go below the sentence, aligned left or right — both are accepted.

Where exactly does it go?

Always the last section of the resume, after skills/hobbies. Never before education or experience.
