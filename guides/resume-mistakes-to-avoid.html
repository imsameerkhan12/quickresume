7 Resume Mistakes That Get You Rejected

Most resumes are rejected in under 10 seconds — often for fixable reasons. Here are the seven that cost people interviews, and how to fix each.

Fix yours with the free builder →

1. Listing duties instead of achievements

"Responsible for managing social media" says nothing. Show impact instead.

✅ "Grew Instagram following from 2k to 15k in 6 months."

2. A cluttered, multi-column design

Fancy templates with sidebars and icons often confuse ATS software, which then drops your resume before a human sees it.

✅ Use a clean single-column layout (all four templates in our builder are ATS-safe).

3. Too long

Freshers should stay to one page; experienced candidates to two. Recruiters skim.

✅ Cut anything older than 10 years or irrelevant to the role.

4. Spelling and grammar errors

They signal carelessness. A single typo can end an application.

✅ Read it aloud, and have one other person proofread it.

5. A generic resume for every job

The same resume sent everywhere rarely matches what any one employer wants.

✅ Adjust your summary and top skills to mirror the job description's keywords.

6. Missing or unprofessional contact info

No LinkedIn, a nickname email, or a wrong phone number quietly kills opportunities.

✅ Use firstname.lastname@… and include a LinkedIn URL.

7. No summary at the top

Recruiters want to know in one line who you are and what role you want.

✅ See how to write a resume summary.
