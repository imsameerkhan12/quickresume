Career Objective for Freshers: 15 Examples That Work

A career objective is the 1–2 line statement at the top of a fresher's resume. Done right, it tells the recruiter instantly what role you want and why you're worth a look. Done wrong, it's empty words ("seeking a challenging position in a reputed organisation…") that get skipped.

The formula

[Your degree/background] + [1–2 real skills] + [the role you want] + [what you'll contribute]. Keep it under 35 words. Avoid clichés like "hardworking", "dynamic", "team player" with no proof.

Engineering / IT freshers

B.Tech (CSE) graduate skilled in Java and SQL, seeking a software developer role where I can build reliable applications and grow into a strong engineer.
Final-year IT student with three React projects on GitHub, looking for a frontend developer position to ship real products from day one.
Mechanical engineering graduate with AutoCAD and SolidWorks training, seeking a design trainee role in a manufacturing company.

B.Com / finance freshers

B.Com graduate with strong Excel and Tally skills, seeking an accounts executive role to manage day-to-day bookkeeping accurately.
Commerce graduate pursuing CA Inter, looking for an audit assistant position to apply my knowledge of accounting standards.

BPO / customer support

Graduate with fluent English and Hindi communication, seeking a customer support role where I can resolve issues quickly and keep customers happy.
12th-pass candidate with 40 WPM typing speed and clear communication, applying for a voice-process BPO role.

MBA freshers

MBA (Marketing) graduate with a summer internship in FMCG sales, seeking a management trainee role to grow revenue in a fast-moving team.
MBA (HR) graduate familiar with recruitment coordination and HRMS tools, looking for an HR executive position.

Other fields

B.Sc. graduate with laboratory experience from college projects, seeking a lab technician role in a diagnostics company.
B.A. English graduate who writes clearly and fast, seeking a content writer role to produce SEO-friendly articles.
B.Ed. holder with student-teaching experience across classes 6–10, seeking a TGT position in a CBSE school.
Hotel management graduate with front-office internship experience, seeking a guest relations role in a 4/5-star property.
Diploma holder in electrical engineering, seeking a maintenance technician role; comfortable with panel wiring and safety procedures.
Recent graduate transitioning to data analysis, with Google Data Analytics certification and strong Excel/SQL basics, seeking a data analyst internship.
✅ Notice the pattern: every example names a real skill and a specific role. Replace the bracketed parts with your truth — never copy one word-for-word.

Where does it go on the resume?

Right below your name and contact details, before education. In our free builder, put it in the Summary box — the templates place it correctly automatically.
