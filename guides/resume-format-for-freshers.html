Best Resume Format for Freshers With No Experience

No job experience yet? That's fine — recruiters expect that from freshers. What they want is a clean, honest resume that highlights your education, projects and skills. Here's the exact section order that works.

The section order that works for freshers

  1. Header — name, phone, professional email, city, LinkedIn/GitHub.
  2. Summary — 1–2 lines: who you are + the role you want. Example: "Final-year Computer Science student seeking a frontend developer role; built 3 React projects and comfortable with JavaScript and Git."
  3. Education — degree, college, year, and percentage/CGPA if strong.
  4. Projects — this replaces work experience. 2–4 projects with what you built, the tools used, and the result.
  5. Skills — languages, tools, and soft skills, comma-separated.
  6. Extras — internships, certifications, volunteering, achievements.

Projects are your secret weapon

When you have no jobs to list, projects prove you can actually do the work. For each project, write one line on what it does and one line on what you did / the outcome. A college project, a personal app, or even a well-documented tutorial you extended all count.

Common fresher mistakes

  • Writing "responsible for…" instead of results.
  • Listing every school subject — keep it to degree-level education.
  • Using a fancy multi-column template that ATS can't read. Keep it simple.
  • An unprofessional email address. Use firstname.lastname@….
